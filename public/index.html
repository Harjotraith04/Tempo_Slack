Why it exists

Slack has no memory of you. It remembers every message, and nothing about which ones were load-bearing. So the work of deciding what matters — the executive function — is silently pushed onto the person least able to spare it.

Tempo is assistive technology for that. It is built for the ~15–20% of knowledge workers who are neurodivergent, for people working in a second language, for anyone coming back from leave to 400 unreads — and, honestly, for everyone else too.

See it work

Five modules, one working memory

Triage

Sorts everything since you were last active into Act / Blocker / FYI / Noise — including the blocker nobody @-mentioned you on.

Commitment Ledger

Every promise you made and every promise made to you, with due dates, drift, and a draft to nudge or renegotiate.

Tone Decoder

Separates what a message literally says from what it means. Names the real urgency, and the social expectation.

Focus Guardian

Books the block: real Do-Not-Disturb, a status your team can see, and calendar time via MCP.

Re-entry

The calm brief you need after time off — decisions made, what changed, who is waiting.

Never auto-sends

Tempo drafts; you send. Nothing leaves your account without your tap — the guarantee that makes it safe to trust.

How it's built

All three hackathon technologies

1

Real-Time Search API

assistant.search.context with the user's own token — so Tempo sees exactly what you can see, and nothing else. It's the only way to ground on the messages that never mentioned you, which is precisely where the blockers hide. What it reads is never stored.

2

MCP — in both directions

Tempo calls MCP servers to book calendar time, and Tempo is an MCP server at /api/mcp/server — exposing tempo_triage, tempo_commitments, tempo_decode, and tempo_focus to Agentforce, Claude, or Cursor, behind signed per-user tokens and a default-deny gate.

3

Slack AI & agent surfaces

The Agent pane (agent_view) with suggested prompts, a live App Home dashboard, Block Kit actions and an accessibility settings modal, four Workflow Builder steps, Tempo Canvas, and Slack Lists.

The privacy posture

Tempo acts as you, on data you already have permission to see. It stores your encrypted token, your preferences, the facts of your commitments, and counts-only metrics. It never persists a single message it reads — that's enforced at the schema level and guarded by tests, not just promised here. You can see everything it holds, export it, and delete it, from the web dashboard.
